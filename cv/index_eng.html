About

I am an industrial engineering (innovation management) graduate (MSc). My passion is development, which includes rethinking software, business operations, operating models, development methods, and more. I consider development to be one of the most critical areas of organizational operations.


”Most innovations fail. And companies that don’t innovate die.” — Henry Chesbrough

I am a software developer with over two decades of experience in various aspects of software development, including requirement specification, architecture design, coding, testing, and functionality design based on service design. I have a profound knowledge of software development and have worked with many different programming languages, such as C/C++, C#, Javascript, SQL, Python, and more. I am familiar with both Linux and Windows operating systems and am particularly interested in hardware-related programming.


Currently, I work at Centria University of Applied Sciences, where I teach information technology to students at the Master’s degree level and work as a project manager in research, development, and innovation projects. As a teacher, I have had to condense my expertise into a form that can be shared with others.


My expertise has been applied in software programs around the world, including pattern recognition, video streaming, database structures, very fast data logging, hardware simulators, PLC interfaces, user interfaces, and more. I am passionate about software development and am always eager to learn new technologies and techniques to improve my skills.
